Prelude

You are one of these explorers. After leaving your home and having no further prospects, you decide to join this bold quest. With high hopes, you sign your life away on an official document and are ushered aboard the Paloma. Suddenly, you’re surrounded by seasoned explorers and warriors with grand stories of their past exploits. There are humans, dwarves, elves, dark elves, and the occasional gnome, and you have never seen such a diverse crowd in your entire life. Before you can change your mind, the port is shrinking into the distance.

Once the ship is on course, Captain Lombardi, the leader of the expedition, introduces himself with a booming voice and states the rules for the voyage. Most of his rules were described in your contract, and any offender to these rules will walk the plank. After he finishes his speech, the captain returns to the stern where he begins speaking with his first mate. You see them holding a gigantic map, and you yearn for a map of your own, wishing you knew the whereabouts of your destination.

While pondering the next chapter of your life, you lean against the ship’s railing and watch the other explorers. You wonder about each of their pasts and from which countries they hailed from. A few of the explorers must be already acquainted since they were either huddling in groups or keeping their distance with solemn expressions. At the moment, nobody seems particularly interested in you, and you feel like that is a good thing.

As sunset approaches, the tensions have lowered, and the explorers have settled down with the crew members to have supper. The elves, all seven of them, entertained the company with a charming story about their midsummer festivals. They detail how midsummer is an essential holiday in their culture and how they felt shameful for missing the celebration of the summer solstice, the longest day of the year. To the elves, this tradition appeased nature and would bring plentiful harvest and prosperity, and they propose having their own festival aboard the ship. That’s when the merrymaking begins.

The elves remove lutes and ocarinas and play elvish ballads while the crew clap their hands and stomp the feet. Meanwhile, you try to eat your supper in relative peace until the elves remove colorful ribbons and transform the mast into a maypole. Before you know it, a female elf hands you a teal ribbon and urges you to celebrate with the others. The only person opposed to the festivities is Captain Lombardi, but even his boisterous laugh echoes over the ship by nightfall. You suspect the seemingly endless kegs of ale from the Paloma’s lower levels.

When you wake in the morning, everything is silent. You walk to the upper decks and study the remains of the previous night. A few of the colorful ribbons are still wrapped around the mast, but the majority of them are strewn across the deck. The first mate orders you to clean up the mess, and you oblige. You were one of the few who had not been drinking ale or wine last night, so at the moment, it was the least you could do. As you reach down to collect a purple ribbon, you stumble as a massive wave slams against the Paloma.

An icy wind billows out of nowhere, and large thunderclouds appear in the distance, roiling toward the ship like a starved beast. The gusts rip the ribbon from your grasp and carry it far away. The grey sky transforms before your eyes, turning as dark as pitch, and you stare upward as the clouds release their burden. Torrential rain pours from above, and you’re swept off your feet by a wave and are nearly dumped overboard. You cling to the railing as the ship comes alive. The crew members scatter to their stations, trying to navigate the Paloma through the harsh storm. A sharp whistling sound fills the air, and you hear malevolent whispers on the wind. As the first bolt of lightning cracks, you’re temporarily blinded by the sudden light, but you can just barely see a host of wispy phantoms surrounding the ship. None of your company seem to notice these creatures, and before you can warn them, you hear a shuddering crack.

A massive hole appears on the vessel’s starboard side, allowing seawater to flood inward. With each passing moment, the ship sinks lower to its watery demise. You make a rash decision and leap overboard. Luckily, there are pieces of driftwood to keep you afloat, and you paddle far away from the sinking ship. Before you can swim more than a few yards, a vortex appears around the ship and sucks it beneath the surface. You cling to the piece of driftwood for dear life as water crashes over your head, submerging you again and again until you fall unconscious. Before the storm disappears, it washes you onto a sandy shore.

Your head feels fuzzy. Blinking your eyes open, you see a fire radiating from a stone hearth. You are tucked beneath warm quilts and feel quite sleepy. Swords and assorted tools mount the walls. You hear a door creak open, and a bearded man enters the room.

"You're awake!"

"Where am I?" you ask, clearing your throat.

The strange man offers you a cup of water which you greatly accept.

"This is my home." The man gestures around him. "I was a delivering a broadsword to Guirag, and on my way back, I found you washed up on the shore. You must have been caught in that evil storm. I tried to wake you, but you were in a deep sleep. I brought you back with me. We're in Sevgard now. You're safe."

After calming down a bit, you fall asleep again. Maybe because of exhaustion, and maybe because of your savior´s soothing words, your mind wanders and you dream. You dream of forests. Lush, beautiful forest untouched by human hand. You walk around in wonder, donning your usual:

  • Sturdy, safe plated armor...
  • Flexible, easy to move in armor...
  • Comfortable, rune-embroidered robes...

Suddenly, you sense presence of danger. You hastily grab your:

  • Sword...
  • Axe...
  • Hammer...
  • Dagger...
  • Bow...
  • Staff...
  • Wand...

You feel surge of killing intent from behind. You leap forward, only to feel claws of beast graze your back. Fortunately you were fast enough. Meanwhile, scenery around you changes. Trees wither, Flowers and leaves are decaying rapidly. Where you sensed serenity and beauty, ther is only death and emptiness now. You sense evil presence all around you. You wake up covered in sweat.

That dream, you feel something odd about it. It felt too real, killing intent was terrifying, you could feel despair of nature. Was it merely heavy nightmare,or premonition? Your heart pounds from shock, and you slowly start to calm down. You realized you clutch bundle of cloth.

„Important, isn´t it?“- old man says

„You clutched it and wouldn´t let go of it for entire 3 days you were out cold“. You hurriedly unpack bundle containing only belongings you managed to pack quickly in storm.

Your armor is all rusty, seawater is harmful to iron after all.

Your leather armor is fine despite its age.

Your precious spell tomes, bundled in your spare cloth, fall apart despite your effort to protect them from water.

Your weapons were old to begin with, but now they are in pathetic state, you need replacements as soon as possible.

As expected, wood didn't survive in storm.

„Are you archer? I can understand your pain of losing bow you made yourself. Here take this one as replacement.“

„Tomes are in tatters? Well, you should ask Serfi, magician living nearby for help.“

Your stomach growls loudly.

„Hungry, are we? Let´s go to kitchen, my wife baked apple pies we can have some“ old man offers.

You follow old man, grateful for his hospitality. After delicious lunch, his wife packs one pie and some peanuts for you to take. You thank her sincerely and turn to leave.

„Here, take some arrows as well, you will need them“ old man gives you one more gift.“

You thank him once more and promise to repay debt.

„Repay? I didn't do it for profit, it did it because it´s right thing to do.“

„To visit Serfi, just follow western path, his dwelling is on top of the hill“. You thank old man once again and head to the west. You arrive at small hut. Suddenly, ground shakes and huge explosion tears small house apart. You rush in worried, only to see old wizard staring angrily at you.

„Your aura distrupted my ritual! Do you want to KILL US ALL YOU IDIOT“ shrieks wizard.

„As fellow student of Magical Arts, you should be aware of risks.“ He keeps ranting. Then he turns around and starts muttering „Adjust the power,yes, I need to reduce impact and concentrate it, hehe....“ You stand there, forgotten and left alone. Just how senile is he. Timidly, you approach him.

„WHAT DO YOU WANT? HAVEN´T YOU DONE ENOUGH DAMAGE ALREADY?“ he roars at you.

You apologize repeatedly and man finally calms down.

„A spare Staff or a Wand, huh? I should have some here, collecting dust along with robes and books for amateurs. Take them and get out of my sight, I don´t need them anyways!!!“ You run off, glad for rags,old tool and your very life.

After all the struggles, you arrive into Sevgard. Not in way you expected, but you begin your life as an adventurer.
